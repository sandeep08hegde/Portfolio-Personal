Segmentation and Prediction of Lung Cancer

This initiative leverages Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN) to enhance early detection and diagnosis of lung cancer, which is marked by the presence of nodules in the lung and is a leading cause of cancer-related deaths. Traditional manual detection through High-Resolution Computed Tomography (HRCT) images is labor-intensive. This project aims to develop a Computer-Aided Detection (CAD) system to assist radiologists in detecting lung nodules more efficiently. It involves collecting chest X-ray images, preprocessing them with geometric mean filtering to improve quality, and using segmentation techniques to isolate regions of interest. The CNN analyzes these images, predicts lung cancer, and provides precise diagnostics by evaluating the softmax probabilities for each class. This integrated approach of image processing and machine learning holds significant potential for improving early detection of lung cancer, enhancing patient survival rates.
